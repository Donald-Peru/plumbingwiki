How to Fix a Leaking Pipe

Posted on November 2, 2025 by Plumbing Wiki Team

Leaking pipes are among the most common plumbing issues homeowners face. Fortunately, many leaks can be handled with simple tools and techniques before professional help is needed.

Step 1: Identify the Source

Begin by turning off the water supply. Carefully inspect joints, fittings, and connections to determine exactly where the leak originates.

Step 2: Apply Temporary Fix

Use plumber’s tape, epoxy putty, or a rubber patch to contain the leak temporarily. These materials can hold for a few days until a permanent repair is made.

“A quick patch is great—but always plan for a full replacement if the pipe is corroded or cracked.”

Step 3: Replace Damaged Section

Cut out the damaged portion using a pipe cutter and replace it with a new section, ensuring you seal joints properly to prevent future leaks.

Pro Tip: Always keep spare couplings and Teflon tape in your toolkit—they can save you hours during an emergency.
